The reading talks abou how it should be a human right to have access to the internet. Blog Post 9

MAKING A FEMINIST INTERNET IN AFRICA: WHY THE INTERNET NEEDS AFRICAN FEMINISTS AND FEMINISMS
by Sheena Magenya

The reading starts off discussing how the pandemic highlighted the fact that there is unequal access to the internet. The author then goes on to talk about how the voices and presence of groups that represent people who typically "exist on the margins of normative societies"(Magenya, 2020) has increased online and the subsequent response this has garnered. Online spaces have reflects the oppression and violence that minority groups experience and often times this harm will be conducted by a small group of people who are very loud. The author then goes on to say that African feminists (as well as other marginalized groups) need to have more presence online and how it is important that these people create online because one it creates knowlegde and provides a resourse for others who may have similar experiences and secondly then these groups can work towards fighting oppression through representing themselves.

The reading also talks about the issue of representation and how it is important that African voices begin to build the narritive around their experience and existence rather than the narritive of Africa being controlled by the west. Again an emphasis is put on the importance of creation, and how in creating itself Africa can shift the narritive. Magenya speaks about how Africa is considered a "beneficiary of technology, not an innovator of it"(Magenya, 2020) but this is not the case it is just that these stories aren't being told but they need to be. The complexity of African lives needs to be expressed and online spaces present a great way of getting these stories and voices to be heard.

Reference: Magenya, S. 2020. Making a Feminist Internet in Africa: Why the internet needs African Feminists and Feminisms. Online. https://genderit.org/editorial/making-feminist-internet-africa-why-internet-needs-african-feminists-and-feminisms
